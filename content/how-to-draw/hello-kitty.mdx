Few characters are as friendly to draw as a round little kitty with a bow. In this guide you'll learn **how to draw Hello Kitty** in a fan-art style, step by step, starting with a single round head and a few light guide lines, then adding the wide-set eyes, button nose, and bow that make this cute kitty drawing so charming. It's one of the best first characters to try because almost every shape is a circle or an oval.

> Not affiliated with or endorsed by the rights holders. This is a fan-style drawing guide that teaches the simple shapes a beginner can use to draw a similar cute kitty character.

Keep your pencil lines light so you can fix the guides later. We'll build the whole figure from big shapes first, then trace and color at the end. Let's begin.

## What you'll need

- A pencil and an eraser
- Plain paper
- Optional: a black pen or marker for outlining, plus crayons or colored pencils

## How to draw Hello Kitty step by step

![Step-by-step: how to draw Hello Kitty in four stages](/images/how-to-draw/hello-kitty/steps.webp)

### Step 1: Draw the round head

Start with a big circle for the head, drawn lightly. A cute kitty character has a head that is almost perfectly round, just a little wider than it is tall. Art teacher Kathy Barbro at [Art Projects for Kids](https://artprojectsforkids.org/how-to-draw-hello-kitty/) builds this character by drawing the circle head first, then adding the smaller parts one at a time. Big shape first is always the easiest place to begin.

### Step 2: Add the ears

On top of the head, draw two pointed cat ears, one on each side. They look like soft triangles with slightly rounded tips. Keep them even in size. A center line dropped lightly down the middle of the face helps you place the ears the same distance from the top.

### Step 3: Place the bow

This kitty's bow sits on the side of the head, usually over the left ear. Draw it as two triangles that meet in the middle, like a small ribbon, with a little knot in the center. The bow is the most recognizable part, so take your time and keep both sides about the same size.

### Step 4: Add the eyes and nose

Draw two small oval eyes low and wide on the face, leaving plenty of space between them. They are simple black dots, with no eyebrows or lashes. Between and a little below the eyes, add a small oval for the nose, usually colored yellow. Wide-set eyes and a tiny nose are what give this cute kitty drawing its sweet, calm look.

### Step 5: Draw the whiskers

Add three short whiskers on each side of the face, spreading out from the cheeks. Keep them straight and evenly spaced. This character has no mouth, which is part of its gentle charm, so leave that area blank.

### Step 6: Add the body and paws

Below the head, draw a simple rounded body for a dress or overalls. Add two short arms with little rounded paws and two stubby legs at the bottom. The body is small compared to the big head, which keeps the look cute and cartoony.

### Step 7: Outline, erase, and color

Trace the lines you want to keep with a firmer stroke or a black pen, then gently erase your center guide line and any stray sketch marks. [Art Projects for Kids](https://artprojectsforkids.org/how-to-draw-hello-kitty/) finishes the same way: trace with a marker, then color. Fill the nose yellow, the bow a bright red or pink, and pick any fun color for the outfit.

## What artists recommend (and common mistakes)

- **Keep your early lines light.** The [Easy Drawing Tutorials](https://easydrawingtutorials.com/index.php/87-hello-kitty/73-draw-hello-kitty) lesson advises sketching with light, smooth strokes so mistakes are easy to erase. The most common beginner mistake is pressing down hard and committing to dark outlines too soon, which makes it tough to fix the symmetry.
- **Build from clear, separate shapes.** Art teacher Kathy Barbro at [Art Projects for Kids](https://artprojectsforkids.org/how-to-draw-hello-kitty/) teaches this character as a stack of simple parts, head, then bow, then dress, then arms and legs. Drawing one shape at a time is far easier than trying to draw the whole figure at once.
- **Watch your spacing.** The eyes should be wide apart and the bow should sit evenly on the head. A quick check in a mirror, or flipping your paper over, makes lopsided spots jump out so you can fix them.

## Fun variations to try

- **A holiday kitty:** Swap the bow for a tiny Santa hat or a flower crown.
- **Different outfits:** Draw a rain coat, a swimsuit, or pajamas to give your kitty a story.
- **Just the face:** Practice the round head, wide eyes, and whiskers on their own until they feel automatic.
- **A whole family:** Make a bigger and a smaller kitty side by side, changing only the bow color.

## Frequently asked questions

**How do you draw Hello Kitty easy?**
Start with a round circle for the head, add two pointed ears and a side bow, then place wide-set oval eyes, a small yellow nose, and three whiskers on each cheek. Add a little body with stubby arms and legs, then trace and color. Building from simple round shapes is what makes it easy.

**Why does Hello Kitty have no mouth?**
In this fan-style guide, we leave the mouth blank because the character is known for its calm, friendly face without one. It also keeps the drawing simpler, which is great for beginners.

**What colors do you use for Hello Kitty?**
A classic look uses a white head and body, a yellow nose, and a red or pink bow. The outfit can be any color you like, so feel free to make it your own.
