    Mandalas are circular designs built on symmetry — patterns that repeat
    evenly around a central point, like the petals of a flower or the spokes of
    a wheel. The word comes from Sanskrit for "circle," and mandalas have been

    cultures. That balanced, repeating structure is exactly what makes them so
    satisfying to color: each ring you fill in mirrors the last, so the page
