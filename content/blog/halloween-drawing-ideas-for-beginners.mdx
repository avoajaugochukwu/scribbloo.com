> Easy Halloween drawing ideas for beginners start from simple shapes. A ghost is one wavy bell shape with two oval eyes. A pumpkin is two curved "parenthesis" lines and a stem. A bat is a rounded body with two scalloped wings. Pick one and you can sketch it in a few minutes.

[Browse more easy drawing ideas for beginners](/blog/drawing-ideas-for-beginners)

If you've ever thought "I can't draw," Halloween is the friendliest place to start. Spooky art lives on basic shapes: circles, triangles, and a few wobbly lines. A ghost is a bean with eyes. A pumpkin is a lumpy circle. Nobody is grading you, and a slightly crooked bat still looks like a bat.

This guide gives you real step-by-step sketches for the five most popular Halloween subjects, a 31-day prompt list to draw all October, cute and spooky variations, and the beginner mistakes worth skipping. Grab a pencil and let's go.


## What you'll need

You don't need a fancy kit. A pencil and one sheet of paper will carry every drawing on this page.

- A pencil. An **HB** pencil is the everyday middle of the grading scale and erases cleanly, which makes it ideal for light guide lines. A **2B** is a little softer and darker, so it's nice for sketchy outlines and quick shading (per the graphite grade guides at [Pencils.com](https://pencils.com/pages/hb-graphite-grading-scale) and [The Virtual Instructor](https://thevirtualinstructor.com/Artists-pencils-graphite-pencils-explained.html)).
- A soft eraser, so you can wipe away the early shapes once the drawing reads.
- A black fine-tip pen or marker, optional, for bold final outlines.
- Crayons, markers, or colored pencils if you want to add color.

Keep your first shapes light. Press gently with the pencil so the guide lines disappear under the eraser later.


## Easy Halloween drawings: 5 to draw step by step

These five build from a circle, an oval, and a couple of curved lines. Walk the steps in order and you'll have a recognizable Halloween cast in minutes.

### How to draw a friendly ghost

A ghost is the easiest spooky thing to draw. It's basically one wavy bell shape.

1. Draw a smooth dome at the top, like the curve of an upside-down U.
2. Carry two soft lines straight down from the dome for the sides.
3. Connect the bottom with a wavy, scalloped line so it looks like a floating bedsheet.
4. Add two oval eyes and a small open mouth.
5. Erase any rough guide lines, then go over your best outline.

Want it cuter? Add two small pink ovals under the eyes for blushing cheeks and a tiny wave with a bean-shaped arm.

### How to draw a jack-o'-lantern pumpkin

A pumpkin is two curved lines and a stem. The face is the fun part.

1. Draw two curved lines side by side, shaped like a wide pair of parentheses `( )`, with a gap in the middle.
2. Add a shorter curved line between them on each side so the pumpkin looks round and ribbed.
3. Cap the top and bottom with gentle curves to close the shape.
4. Draw a short, chunky stem at the top.
5. Add two triangle eyes, a triangle nose, and a wide zigzag smile to carve the face.

Fun fact to sketch around: the first jack-o'-lanterns weren't pumpkins at all. People in Ireland and Scotland carved scary faces into turnips and other root vegetables, and the pumpkin version came later in America (per [HISTORY](https://www.history.com/articles/history-of-the-jack-o-lantern-irish-origins)). Try drawing a lumpy turnip lantern for a twist nobody else will have.

### How to draw a bat

A bat is a rounded body and two wings with a scalloped edge.

1. Draw a small rounded body, a little taller than it is wide.
2. Add two pointed ears at the top, like a cat's.
3. From each side of the body, draw a wing: go up to a point, then come back to the body with two or three scalloped bumps along the bottom edge.
4. Add two round eyes and a tiny smile.
5. Erase the guide lines and darken your outline.

A purple bat with a smile reads as cute, not creepy, so color it in if you want a friendlier one.

### How to draw a spider

A spider is one oval and eight legs, four on each side.

1. Draw a small oval for the body.
2. On the left side, draw four legs. Make each leg bend at a sharp angle, like an upside-down V.
3. Repeat on the right side so you have four legs per side, eight in total.
4. Add two small round eyes near the top of the body.
5. Erase your guides and ink the final lines.

Counting to eight as you draw the legs keeps the spider from ending up with too many or too few.

### How to draw a spider web

A spider web is a set of straight lines with curved threads jumping between them.

1. Draw a plus sign: one vertical line and one horizontal line that cross in the middle.
2. Add two diagonal lines across the same center so you have eight spokes, like an asterisk.
3. Near the center, draw a short curved line that arcs from one spoke to the next.
4. Keep arcing from spoke to spoke, working outward in rings, each ring a little farther from the center.
5. Stop when you reach the ends of the spokes, then add your spider hanging from one thread.

The slight sag in each curved arc is what makes it read as a web instead of a wheel, so let those lines dip a little.


## Halloween doodle ideas: spooky flair for your notes

Doodling is drawing's laid-back cousin. No rules, no pressure, just pen meeting paper in the margin of a planner or a homework page. Halloween doodles are perfect for this because the shapes are so simple.

Try these tiny ones:

- A witch hat: a triangle with a wide curved brim.
- A candy corn: a triangle in three stripes.
- A single eyeball with a few wavy veins.
- A row of bunting bats strung along the top of a page.
- A ghost peeking over the edge of your to-do list.

Keep doodles small so they fit in corners, repeat one simple shape with different faces, and add playful touches like a bow or a speech bubble. The goal is fun, not fine art, so let them be a little wonky.

## Cute Halloween drawings: the adorable side of spooky

Halloween doesn't have to be scary. Soft round shapes, big eyes, and a bit of silliness turn anything spooky into something sweet, which is exactly what beginners and younger kids tend to enjoy most.

Cute ideas to try:

- A pumpkin cupcake with a candle on top.
- A baby ghost holding a lollipop.
- A bat in a tiny knitted sweater.
- A skeleton with heart-shaped eyes.
- A black cat curled into a sleepy circle.

The style tricks are simple. Use round shapes instead of sharp ones, draw thick friendly outlines, add blush cheeks and oversized eyes, and keep the details few. A spider that looks shy is just a regular spider with smaller, lower eyes and a tiny smile. Cute isn't childish, it's a real style loved by artists of every age.


## Beginner mistakes to avoid

No one starts as an expert, so here are the slip-ups worth dodging.

- **Chasing perfect.** A wobbly ghost still reads as a ghost. Start simple and let the early sketches be rough.
- **Jumping into a full scene.** Draw one object before a whole graveyard. One pumpkin teaches you more than a crowded scene you abandon halfway.
- **Pressing too hard on guide lines.** Keep early shapes light so they erase cleanly under your final outline.
- **Only copying.** Use a reference, then change something so it becomes yours: a new face, a new color, a sillier expression.
- **Waiting to feel "ready."** You get good by starting, not by feeling talented first. Drawing is a skill that grows with practice.

Pick a theme for the day, keep it small, and laugh at the weird ones. That's the whole method.


## Frequently asked questions

### What is the easiest Halloween thing to draw?

A ghost is the easiest Halloween thing to draw. It's one wavy bell shape with two oval eyes and a small mouth, so you can finish it in under a minute. A candy corn (a triangle in three color bands) and a single jack-o'-lantern are close runners-up for total beginners.

### What can a 7-year-old draw for Halloween?

A 7-year-old can draw a friendly ghost, a smiling pumpkin, a bat, or a black cat using basic shapes. Start with light pencil guide lines, keep each drawing to one object, and add a happy face so it stays fun rather than scary. The 31-day prompt list above works well as a simple daily activity.

### What tools do I need for Halloween drawing?

A pencil and paper are all you need. An HB pencil keeps guide lines light and easy to erase, and a softer 2B is nice for darker outlines. Add a black fine-tip pen for bold final lines and crayons, markers, or colored pencils for color. For digital art, beginner-friendly apps work the same way with an undo button.

### How do you draw a scary Halloween picture?

Make a cute subject scarier by changing a few details. Use sharp triangle shapes instead of round ones, add jagged teeth and narrow angry eyes, and draw heavier dark shading on one side. A haunted house with crooked windows, a bare spooky tree, and a thin crescent moon set a creepy mood without needing advanced skill.
