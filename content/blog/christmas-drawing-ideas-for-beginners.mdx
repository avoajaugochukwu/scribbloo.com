> Easy Christmas drawing ideas for beginners start from simple shapes. A tree is a stack of three triangles. A snowman is three circles. A candy cane is a tall "J". An ornament is a circle with a little cap. Pick one and you can sketch it in a few minutes.

If you can hold a pencil, you can draw something festive this Christmas. Holiday art lives on basic shapes: circles for ornaments, triangles for trees, a couple of "U" curves for a candy cane. A wobbly snowman still looks like a snowman, and nobody is grading you.

This guide gives you real step-by-step sketches for the most popular Christmas subjects, a set of ideas just for handmade cards, cute holiday doodles, a 30-day December prompt list, and the beginner mistakes worth skipping. Grab a pencil and let's get sketching.


## What you'll need

You don't need a fancy kit. A pencil and one sheet of paper will carry every drawing on this page.

- A pencil. An **HB** pencil is the everyday middle of the grading scale and erases cleanly, which makes it ideal for light guide lines. A **2B** is a little softer and darker, so it's nice for sketchy outlines and quick shading (per the graphite grade guides at [Pencils.com](https://pencils.com/pages/hb-graphite-grading-scale) and [The Virtual Instructor](https://thevirtualinstructor.com/Artists-pencils-graphite-pencils-explained.html)).
- A soft eraser, so you can wipe away the early shapes once the drawing reads.
- A black fine-tip pen or marker, optional, for bold final outlines. Let the ink dry before you erase the pencil underneath.
- Crayons, markers, or colored pencils for the festive colors. Red, green, and a warm gold cover most of Christmas.

Keep your first shapes light. Press gently with the pencil so the guide lines disappear under the eraser later.


## Easy Christmas drawings: 6 to draw step by step

These six build from a triangle, a circle, and a couple of curved lines. Walk the steps in order and you'll have a recognizable Christmas cast in minutes.

### How to draw a Christmas tree

A Christmas tree is a stack of three triangles. The decorations are the fun part.

1. Draw a wide, short triangle near the bottom of your page, but leave the very bottom edge open.
2. Above it, draw a slightly narrower triangle that overlaps the top of the first, again leaving its bottom edge open.
3. Add one more narrow triangle on top the same way, so the three stack into a layered pine.
4. Draw a small rectangle under the bottom triangle for the trunk.
5. Add a five-pointed star at the very top, then dot on circles for ornaments and a few looping lines for garland.

Keep the triangles a little uneven and the ornaments different sizes. A perfectly symmetrical tree looks stiff, while a slightly lopsided one looks hand-drawn and warm.

### How to draw a snowman

A snowman is three circles, stacked from big to small. Everything else is an accessory.

1. Draw a large circle near the bottom for the base.
2. Draw a medium circle resting on top of it, slightly overlapping.
3. Draw a small circle on top of that for the head.
4. Add two dot eyes, a triangle carrot nose, and a curved row of small dots for a smiling mouth.
5. Finish with two stick arms, three buttons down the front, and a hat or scarf so it doesn't look bare.

The classic snowman face is two coal eyes, a carrot nose, and a smile, so those three details are what turn three plain circles into a character.

### How to draw a candy cane

A candy cane is a tall "J", and the stripes do the rest.

1. Draw a long curved line that hooks over at the top, like a walking cane or the letter J flipped around.
2. Draw a second line running parallel to the first, the same distance apart the whole way, and close off both ends so it reads as a solid cane.
3. Add diagonal stripes across the cane, all slanting the same direction so they look like they wrap around it.
4. Color every other stripe red and leave the rest white.
5. Tie a small bow where the straight part meets the hook if you want a card-ready version.

The shape echoes a shepherd's crook. A recipe for peppermint candy sticks appears in the 1844 cookbook *The Complete Confectioner* (per [Wikipedia's sourced summary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Candy_cane)), but the red-and-white stripes we picture today only emerged around the turn of the 20th century and were not mass-produced until the 1920s (per [HISTORY](https://www.history.com/articles/candy-canes-invented-germany)).

### How to draw a Christmas ornament

A Christmas ornament, or bauble, is a circle with a tiny cap on top. It's the easiest place to get creative.

1. Draw a circle. A bauble can be round, oval, or even teardrop-shaped, so pick whatever you like.
2. Add a small rectangle at the top for the metal cap.
3. Draw a little loop or hook above the cap, like the top of a paperclip, so it can hang.
4. Decorate inside the circle with patterns: stripes, dots, zigzags, snowflakes, or a wavy band across the middle.
5. Color it in, leaving a small white oval near the top edge so it looks shiny.

That little white highlight is the trick that makes a flat circle look like a glass ball, so don't color over it.

### How to draw a gingerbread man

A gingerbread man is one rounded body and a friendly face. Think of a flattened person made of cookie dough.

1. Draw a circle for the head.
2. Under it, draw a rounded body shape that's a little wider at the bottom, like a bell.
3. Add two arms curving out and up from the sides, and two short legs at the bottom, all with rounded ends.
4. Draw two dot eyes and a smiling mouth, then two small dots on the cheeks for blush.
5. Add the icing details: a squiggly line across each wrist and ankle, two or three buttons down the front, and a zigzag collar.

Keep every edge soft and rounded. Gingerbread has no sharp corners, so curved lines are what make it read as a cookie instead of a person.

### How to draw a reindeer

A reindeer is a circle face with antlers, and the red nose seals the deal.

1. Draw a circle for the head, then a slightly pointed oval underneath for the snout.
2. Add two long oval ears, one on each side near the top of the head.
3. From behind the ears, draw two branching antlers. Make each one a curved line with two or three smaller lines branching off, like bare twigs.
4. Add two round eyes, then a big circle on the tip of the snout for the nose.
5. Color the nose red, add two pink ovals for rosy cheeks, and erase your guide lines.

Counting the antler branches as you go keeps them even. Two or three points on each side is plenty, and matching them roughly left to right keeps the face balanced.


## Christmas card drawing ideas

Handmade cards are the easiest way to put a quick drawing to use, and the best card designs are simple. A single subject in the middle with a short greeting underneath beats a crowded scene every time.

Try these card-friendly ideas:

- A single ornament with a hand-lettered "Merry Christmas" below it.
- Three candy canes crossed together with a bow in the center.
- A sprig of holly: two pointed leaves and three red berry circles in the corner.
- A row of small triangle trees along the bottom edge, like a tiny forest.
- One snowman waving, with a few dot snowflakes falling around him.
- A wreath: a ring of small green leaf shapes with a red bow at the bottom.

Leave room for the greeting before you start drawing, sketch the design in pencil first, then ink the lines and erase the guides once the ink is dry. A little white space around the drawing makes a card look finished instead of cramped.


## Cute Christmas doodles for journals and notes

Doodling is drawing's laid-back cousin. No rules, no pressure, just pen meeting paper in the margin of a planner or a bullet journal. Christmas doodles work well here because the shapes are so small and simple.

Try these tiny ones:

- A mug of cocoa: a "U" shape with a handle, two little marshmallow circles, and a wavy steam line.
- A single mitten: a rounded rectangle with a thumb bump on the side.
- A string of round lights along the top of a page.
- A present: a square with a ribbon cross and two loops for the bow.
- A snowflake: one line crossed by two more, with little forks on the ends.

Keep doodles small so they fit in corners, repeat one simple shape with different faces or patterns, and add playful touches like blush cheeks or a tiny bow. The goal is fun, not fine art, so let them be a little wonky.

## Cute Christmas drawings: the cozy, kawaii side

Christmas art doesn't have to be polished. Soft round shapes, big eyes, and a bit of warmth turn any festive object into something sweet, which is exactly what beginners and younger kids tend to enjoy most.

Cute ideas to try:

- A round penguin in a knitted scarf.
- A baby reindeer with oversized eyes and a tiny red nose.
- A mug of cocoa with a marshmallow snowman peeking out.
- A present with a face and little arms, hugging a candy cane.
- A polar bear curled into a sleepy circle wearing a Santa hat.

The style tricks are simple. Use round shapes instead of sharp ones, draw thick friendly outlines, add two small blush ovals on the cheeks, give it big eyes set a little low, and keep the details few. Cute isn't childish, it's a real style loved by artists of every age.


## How to draw an ugly Christmas sweater

The ugly Christmas sweater has become its own little drawing trend, and it's forgiving by design. The whole point is loud, mismatched patterns, so there's no wrong way to do it.

1. Draw a rounded rectangle for the body of the sweater, tapering in slightly at the waist.
2. Add two sleeves angling out and down from the top corners.
3. Draw a ribbed collar at the neck and ribbed cuffs at the wrists and bottom hem using short stacked lines.
4. Fill the front with one big motif: a reindeer face, a tree, or a snowman.
5. Crowd the rest with smaller patterns: zigzags, dots, candy canes, snowflakes, and clashing color blocks.

The "ugly" charm comes from overdoing it, so pack in more patterns and brighter color clashes than you normally would. This one is great for kids and for a quick festive doodle on a card.


## Beginner mistakes to avoid

No one starts as an expert, so here are the slip-ups worth dodging.

- **Chasing perfect.** A lopsided tree still reads as a tree. Start simple and let the early sketches be rough.
- **Jumping into a full scene.** Draw one ornament before a whole mantelpiece. One snowman teaches you more than a crowded scene you abandon halfway.
- **Pressing too hard on guide lines.** Keep early shapes light so they erase cleanly under your final outline.
- **Skipping the highlight.** On ornaments and baubles, leaving a small white spot is what makes them look shiny instead of flat.
- **Waiting to feel "ready."** You get good by starting, not by feeling talented first. Drawing is a skill that grows with practice.

Pick one subject for the day, keep it small, and laugh at the weird ones. That's the whole method.


## Frequently asked questions

### What is the easiest Christmas thing to draw?

A candy cane is one of the easiest Christmas things to draw. It's a tall "J" shape with diagonal stripes, so you can finish it in under a minute. A single ornament (a circle with a little cap) and a three-triangle Christmas tree are close runners-up for total beginners.

### What should I draw on a Christmas card?

Keep a Christmas card simple: one clear subject in the middle with a short greeting underneath. A single ornament, a sprig of holly, three crossed candy canes, or a row of small triangle trees all work well. Sketch in pencil first, leave space for the words, then ink and erase the guides.

### What can kids draw for Christmas?

Kids can draw a snowman, a Christmas tree, a candy cane, a reindeer, or a gingerbread man using basic shapes. Start with light pencil guide lines, keep each drawing to one object, and add a happy face so it stays fun. The 30-day prompt list above works well as a simple daily December activity.

### How do you draw a simple Christmas tree?

Draw a stack of three triangles, each a little narrower than the one below and slightly overlapping. Add a small rectangle at the bottom for the trunk and a five-pointed star on top. Then dot on circles for ornaments and a few looping lines for garland. Keep the triangles a touch uneven so it looks hand-drawn.
