> Looking for unicorn activities for kids? Here are 15 screen-free ideas grouped by type — free printables, easy crafts, party games, and learning fun. Each one is low-prep, uses supplies you likely have at home, and works for toddlers through early elementary. Pick one and start the magic today.

Want the easiest starting point? Print a few free [unicorn coloring pages](/coloring-pages/fantasy/unicorn) and build your whole magical afternoon around them.

## How to use this list

These 15 unicorn activities for kids are sorted into four groups so you can grab what fits the moment: **printables** for quiet time, **crafts** for a make-something afternoon, **party games** for a birthday or playdate, and **learning activities** for the classroom or homeschool table.

Most ideas take a few minutes to set up and use things already in your craft drawer — paper, glue, crayons, popsicle sticks. Where an activity suits a certain age, we say so, since a 3-year-old and an 8-year-old want very different levels of challenge. And if you want a ready-made theme day, National Unicorn Day on April 9 is a fun hook to plan around. National Day Calendar notes the observance took off around 2015 alongside the unicorn's pop-culture comeback, with no single confirmed founder, per [National Day Calendar](https://www.nationaldaycalendar.com/national-day/national-unicorn-day-april-9).

## Printable unicorn fun

The lowest-prep ideas of all. Print, hand over a few crayons or colored pencils, and you have a calm, screen-free stretch.

### 1. Free unicorn coloring pages

Nothing sparks creativity like a blank unicorn waiting to be colored. Coloring builds fine motor control, color recognition, and patience, and it suits every age at once: hand younger kids the simple, bold-outline pages and let older kids tackle the detailed fantasy scenes.

To stretch it further, name the parts as you go (horn, mane, hooves, tail) to build vocabulary for preschoolers. Finished pages can become greeting cards, bookmarks, or fridge art. Print on A4 or US Letter and choose "fit to page" so nothing gets cropped. Crayons and colored pencils work best on standard 20 lb / 75 GSM printer paper. If you want to use markers, print on cardstock (about 65 lb / 176 GSM) so the color doesn't bleed through.

### 2. Unicorn dot-to-dot printables

Dot-to-dot sheets teach number sequencing while building focus. Start beginners with connect-the-dots from 1 to 20, then move older kids up to 50 or more. When the picture appears, they color it in, which doubles the activity.

For a magical twist, have kids switch crayon colors as they connect, so the finished outline is already a rainbow. Laminate the sheets (or slip them into a clear sleeve) and use dry-erase markers to reuse them again and again.

### 3. Unicorn maze and puzzle sheets

Printable mazes and puzzles keep kids challenged and on-theme. Try a maze where a unicorn has to find its rainbow, a "match the unicorn horns" puzzle, or simple picture riddles.

These build problem-solving and visual tracking. Staple a few sheets together to make a little unicorn puzzle workbook, and reward a finished booklet with a sticker or a coloring page they get to choose.

## Crafty unicorn creations

Hands-on makes for a different kind of afternoon. These crafts build scissor skills, hand strength, and a lot of pride in the finished thing.

### 4. DIY unicorn horn headbands

Part craft, part costume. Roll a piece of glitter cardstock into a cone, stuff it lightly with cotton or tissue so it holds its shape, and seal the seam with tape or glue. Attach the cone to a plain headband or an elastic band, then let kids decorate around it with paper flowers, gems, or yarn.

This one is great for scissor practice and fine motor control. Theme the horn by color if you like — a pink "love unicorn" or a blue "ocean unicorn."

### 5. Paper plate unicorn masks

Turn a paper plate into a wearable unicorn. Cut two eye holes, draw a sweet face, and glue on paper ears, a horn, and strands of yarn or ribbon for the mane. Add a craft-stick handle, or staple on elastic to wear it hands-free.

Set up a quick unicorn photo booth and let kids pose with their masks. Short on time? Pre-cut the ears and horn so the focus stays on decorating.

### 6. Unicorn jar nightlight

Make a glowing jar that doubles as room decor. Fill a clean jar with pink, purple, and white cotton balls, add a pinch of glitter, and drop in a battery-operated LED tea light. Use a battery light, not a real flame. Seal the lid and enjoy the soft glow.

This calming, tactile craft is a lovely wind-down before bed. Talk about how the light scatters through the cotton for a simple, gentle science tie-in.

### 7. Unicorn slime

Glittery pastel slime is a sensory favorite, and you can make it safely without borax powder. Stir together washable school glue, a little baking soda, and saline contact-lens solution as the activator, then add a drop of pastel food coloring and some iridescent glitter. Make separate pink, purple, and blue batches and gently swirl them together.

One thing matters for the slime to set: the saline solution has to list **boric acid** or **sodium borate** in its ingredients — that mild activator is far safer than pure borax powder, [as Kids Activities Blog explains](https://kidsactivitiesblog.com/107068/borax-free-slime-recipes/). Always supervise, keep slime out of mouths and away from very young kids who might taste it, and have everyone wash their hands when they finish.

### 8. Unicorn stick puppets

Glue a printed and colored unicorn onto a craft stick, then add yarn hair, googly eyes, and a little sparkle. Encourage kids to invent characters with names, personalities, and backstories.

Once the cast is ready, stage a puppet show behind a draped blanket or a cardboard-box theater. This builds storytelling, confidence, and a surprising amount of plot.

## Unicorn party games

Built for a birthday, a playdate, or a unicorn theme day. These get kids moving and laughing together.

### 9. Unicorn ring toss

Make a unicorn horn from a cardstock cone (the same one from the headband craft works) and mount it on a stable base, like a weighted box or a sand-filled bottle. Give kids soft rings or glow-stick loops to toss onto the horn.

It is great for hand-eye coordination, and you can split players into teams and keep score for a little friendly competition.

### 10. Pin the horn on the unicorn

Print or draw a big unicorn without its horn and tape it to the wall. Cut several colorful paper horns and put a loop of painter's tape or a sticky dot on each. Blindfold each player, give them a gentle spin, and let them try to place the horn.

When everyone has had a turn, snap a photo of the wonderfully wrong result. For an older group, have kids write their names on their horns so you can see who landed closest.

### 11. Unicorn bingo

Make bingo cards using unicorn-themed pictures — stars, cupcakes, rainbows, crystals — and use candy, buttons, or pom-poms as markers. Print several different layouts so no two cards match.

Call the items out in silly voices for extra giggles. Laminate the cards or use clear sleeves if you will play more than once, especially in a classroom.

### 12. DIY unicorn obstacle course

Set up a simple course indoors or in the yard. Kids jump over rainbows (pool noodles), crawl under cloud tunnels (a bedsheet over two chairs), and toss bean bags into "enchanted" baskets.

Name each station for the fun of it — "leap the glitter river," "dash through the candy-cane forest." Hand out homemade "Unicorn Explorer" certificates (a coloring page works) when they finish the course.

## Unicorn learning activities

Quiet, brainy, and easy to slot into a school or homeschool day. Each one hides a real skill inside the fun.

### 13. Unicorn word search

Make or print a unicorn word search with age-right vocabulary like sparkle, rainbow, wings, horn, and magic. Offer an easier version and a harder one so a mixed-age group can play together.

When the search is done, ask kids to use each word in a sentence or draw a quick picture of it. That small step turns a puzzle into a language-arts win.

### 14. Unicorn reading hour

Build a cozy nook with pillows, a blanket, and a unicorn plushie, then read books that celebrate imagination and being yourself. A few well-loved titles:

- [_Uni the Unicorn_](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/series/UNI/uni-the-unicorn/) by Amy Krouse Rosenthal
- [_Not Quite Narwhal_](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/Not-Quite-Narwhal/Jessie-Sima/Not-Quite-Narwhal-and-Friends/9781481469098) by Jessie Sima
- [_Thelma the Unicorn_](https://shop.scholastic.com/teachers-ecommerce/teacher/books/thelma-the-unicorn-9781338158427.html) by Aaron Blabey

Pair the reading with a coloring page, or have kids retell the story in their own words for a comprehension boost.

### 15. Unicorn color-by-number math

Color-by-number sheets sneak math into art. Kids solve simple problems — addition, subtraction, or early multiplication — and the answers tell them which color goes where on the unicorn.

These are perfect for math centers, early finishers, or homework that doesn't feel like homework. Offer a few difficulty levels, and add a bonus "challenge" question that unlocks an extra detail to color.

## Tips for a magical unicorn day

A few small things make these activities go more smoothly:

- **Prep the messy ones first.** Lay out everything for slime or painting before you call the kids over.
- **Keep crayons and colored pencils handy.** They are the lowest-mess way to fill any pause between activities.
- **Use cardstock for anything markers touch.** Around 65 lb / 176 GSM stops bleed-through on coloring pages and masks.
- **Save the finished art.** A binder or a wall display turns a one-off afternoon into something kids feel proud of.

## Frequently asked questions

### What are good unicorn activities for a kids' birthday party?

Set up a coloring station with free printable unicorn pages, run a few easy games like unicorn ring toss and pin the horn on the unicorn, and offer a craft such as decorate-your-own horn headbands as a party favor. Rotate kids through the stations so everyone stays busy.

### How do you make safe unicorn slime for kids?

Mix washable school glue, a little baking soda, and saline contact-lens solution as the activator, then add pastel food coloring and glitter. The saline must list boric acid or sodium borate to work, which is much milder than pure borax powder. Always supervise, keep it out of mouths, and wash hands after.

### What unicorn activities work for preschoolers and toddlers?

Stick to bold-outline coloring pages, paper plate unicorn masks with pre-cut pieces, sensory play, and counting games with unicorn cutouts. Keep crafts simple and skip small parts or sharp tools for very young children. The cutting and detailed dot-to-dots are better saved for early elementary kids.

### When is National Unicorn Day?

National Unicorn Day is April 9 each year. According to National Day Calendar, the observance took off around 2015 as unicorns surged back into pop culture, and no single founder is confirmed. It makes a fun anchor for a unicorn-themed activity day at home or in the classroom.
