## Introduction


Unicorns are more than just a trend — they’ve become a full-blown obsession for many kids. From backpacks to birthday cakes, these magical creatures spark joy and imagination wherever they appear. But if you're a parent, teacher, or caregiver looking for screen-free unicorn activities, you're in the right place.


This post shares 15 magical unicorn-themed ideas that are fun, low-prep, and packed with creativity. You'll also find free unicorn printables like coloring pages and word games to keep little ones engaged.


## Printable Fun


### Activity 1: Free Unicorn Coloring Pages


Nothing sparks creativity like a blank unicorn ready to be colored in. Coloring helps develop fine motor skills, color recognition, and patience in kids. Offer a mix of simple outlines for younger children and more detailed scenes for older ones who enjoy a challenge. Provide themed sets like “Unicorns in Nature,” “Fairyland Unicorns,” or “Unicorns and Friends.”


To extend the activity, label parts of the unicorn (horn, mane, hooves) to build vocabulary for preschoolers. Kids can also turn their completed pages into greeting cards, bookmarks, or fridge art.

### Activity 2: Unicorn Dot-to-Dot Printables


Dot-to-dot printables are great for teaching number sequencing and improving concentration. Start with connect-the-dots from 1 to 20 for beginners, and go up to 50 or more for older kids. Once completed, kids can color their unicorn creation.


A fun variation is using rainbow-colored pencils to connect the dots, adding a bit of visual magic to the exercise. Laminate the sheets for reuse with dry-erase markers, especially in classroom settings.


### Activity 3: Unicorn Maze and Puzzle Sheets


These printable puzzles keep kids challenged while staying on-theme. Create mazes where a unicorn has to find its rainbow, or logic puzzles where kids must match unicorn horns or solve simple riddles.


This activity builds problem-solving and visual tracking skills. You can staple a few sheets into a booklet to create a unicorn puzzle workbook. Reward completion with stickers or small prizes to keep kids motivated.


## Crafty Creations


### Activity 4: DIY Unicorn Horn Headbands


Unicorn horn headbands are equal parts costume and craft. Roll glitter cardstock into a cone and stuff it with cotton to help it keep its shape. Attach it to an elastic band and let kids decorate with flowers, gems, or colorful yarn.


This craft is ideal for building hand strength, scissor skills, and creativity. You can also theme the horns by color — for example, a pink horn for a “Love Unicorn” or blue for an “Ocean Unicorn.”


### Activity 5: Paper Plate Unicorn Masks


Transform paper plates into wearable unicorn masks. Cut eye holes, draw a smiling face, and glue on ears, a horn, and yarn or ribbon for the mane. Use a popsicle stick as a handle or attach elastic to wear it hands-free.


Set up a unicorn-themed photo booth and let kids take pictures wearing their masks. If you're short on time, pre-cut pieces so the focus stays on decorating.


### Activity 6: Unicorn Jar Nightlight


Create a glowing jar that doubles as magical room decor. Fill a mason jar with pink, purple, and white cotton balls, sprinkle in glitter, and add a battery-operated tea light inside. Seal the jar and enjoy the ambient glow.


This sensory craft is calming and tactile, perfect for winding down before bed. Talk to kids about how light diffuses through the cotton or how shadows form inside the jar as a simple STEM tie-in.


### Activity 7: Unicorn Slime


Make glittery unicorn slime using glue, baking soda, saline solution, and food coloring. Create separate batches in pastel shades (pink, purple, blue), then swirl them together. Sprinkle in iridescent glitter or star confetti for extra sparkle.


This is a great sensory experience, but be sure to supervise the process and have kids wash their hands afterward. You can talk about how slime is a polymer to add an educational layer. Use vinegar for any cleanup if slime sticks to surfaces.


### Activity 8: Unicorn Stick Puppets


Turn cardstock or printed coloring pages into stick puppets. Glue them to popsicle sticks, add yarn hair, googly eyes, and a bit of sparkle. Encourage kids to create different unicorn characters with names, traits, and backstories.


Once complete, invite them to perform a puppet show using a cardboard box theater or simply behind a blanket. This activity builds storytelling skills, confidence, and creativity.


## Party & Games


### Activity 9: Unicorn Ring Toss Game


Create a unicorn horn from a cone or rolled cardstock and mount it on a stable base. Provide kids with soft rings or glow stick loops to toss onto the horn.


This is a fantastic party game that encourages hand-eye coordination and gross motor skills. You can even divide kids into teams and keep score for friendly competition. Keep extra decorations nearby so kids can decorate their rings, too.


### Activity 10: Pin the Horn on the Unicorn


Print or draw a large unicorn image without the horn. Cut out multiple colorful horn shapes and use painter's tape or Velcro dots to attach them. Blindfold kids, spin them around, and let them try their luck.


After everyone’s had a turn, take a photo of the “finished” unicorn with all the misplaced horns. For an educational twist, have kids write their names on their horns and measure how far off they were from the target.


### Activity 11: Unicorn Bingo


Design bingo cards using unicorn-themed images like stars, cupcakes, rainbows, and crystals. Use candy, buttons, or sparkly stones as markers. Print out several unique layouts to avoid overlap.


For added fun, call out the items using character voices or silly descriptions. Laminate the cards for durability, especially if you'll use them multiple times in a classroom or club setting.


### Activity 12: DIY Unicorn Obstacle Course


Set up a simple obstacle course in your living room, yard, or school gym. Kids can jump over rainbows (pool noodles), crawl under cloud tunnels (bedsheets draped over chairs), and toss bean bags into enchanted baskets.


Each checkpoint can be unicorn-themed — for example, “Leap across the Glitter River” or “Dash through the Candy Cane Forest.” Create printed “Unicorn Explorer” certificates as a prize for completing the course.


## Learning Activities


### Activity 13: Unicorn Word Search (Printable)


Create or download a unicorn-themed word search with age-appropriate vocabulary such as sparkle, rainbow, wings, horn, and magic. Offer both simple and challenging versions.


After completing the word search, ask kids to use each word in a sentence or draw an image that represents it. This adds an element of language arts to the fun.


### Activity 14: Unicorn-Themed Reading Hour


Set up a cozy nook with pillows, blankets, and unicorn plushies. Choose books that celebrate imagination, bravery, and self-acceptance. Some great titles include:

- [_Uni the Unicorn_](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/series/UNI/uni-the-unicorn/) by Amy Krouse Rosenthal
- [_Not Quite Narwhal_](https://www.netflix.com/ca/title/81296780) by Jessie Sima
- [_Thelma the Unicorn_](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt10442352/) by Aaron Blabey

Pair reading with a coloring activity or ask kids to retell the story in their own words for a comprehension boost.


### Activity 15: Unicorn Math Worksheet (Color-by-Number)


Use color-by-number worksheets that combine math and art. Kids solve problems — such as simple addition or multiplication — and use the results to color sections of a unicorn drawing.


These worksheets are excellent for math centers, homework, or early finishers. Offer different difficulty levels to keep kids challenged and engaged. Consider adding bonus “challenge” questions that unlock extra decorations for the unicorn.
