Drawing is most fun when there's no pressure and no grade at the end. The goal is to relax, smile, and fill the page with happy shapes. Below are 50 **things to draw for fun**, sorted into easy themes, each with the simple shape to start from. These **fun drawing ideas** work for any age and any skill level, so you don't need talent or fancy supplies. Pick the first one that makes you grin, keep your lines loose, and just play. There's no wrong way to do this, and you've got this.

## How to use this list

Pick one idea and start from its basic shape. Most of these are a circle, an oval, or a few lines with silly details on top. Draw lightly at first so you can change your mind, then darken the lines you love. Don't aim for perfect. The wobbly, goofy version is usually the most fun. Skip around, repeat your favorites, and color whenever you feel like it.

## Funny doodles to draw

Silly is the whole point here. These **drawing ideas funny** enough to make you laugh while you draw.

### A potato with legs
A lumpy oval with two stick legs and a goofy face. The lumpier it is, the funnier.

### An avocado with a face
An avocado outline with a big round pit and two surprised eyes. Add tiny arms for extra silliness.

### A grumpy cloud
A puffy cloud shape with frowny eyes and one little raincloud frown. Add a tiny lightning bolt.

### A donut with a face
A circle with a hole, a frosting drip, and two happy eyes peeking over the top.

### A taco with arms
A folded taco shape with two stick arms throwing up a cheer. Add veggie squiggles inside.

### A wobbly robot
A boxy body, a square head, and bendy spring arms. Crooked antennas make it cuter.

### A cat loaf
A cat tucked into a bread-loaf shape with no legs showing. Just a dome with ears and a face.

### A blob monster
One big bean shape, three eyes, and a couple of small teeth. There are no rules for blobs.

## Fun animals to draw

Animals are just rounded shapes with a face, and that makes them perfect for playful drawing.

### A round cat
A circle for the head, two triangle ears, and a smiley face. Big low eyes read as friendly.

### A happy dog
An oval head with two floppy ears and a round nose. Try our full [how to draw a dog](/how-to-draw/dog) guide for a finished look.

### A spider with a smile
Two body circles and eight bendy legs, plus a cheery face. See [how to draw a spider](/how-to-draw/spider) for the steps.

### A penguin
An egg shape with a white belly, two flippers, and little orange feet. Add a tiny scarf.

### A puffy owl
A rounded body with two big circle eyes and a triangle beak. Add small feather lines on the chest.

### A fox
A rounded head with two pointy ears and a triangle nose. A big fluffy tail finishes it.

### A chubby bunny
A round body, a round head, two tall ears, and a tiny puff tail. Keep everything soft and round.

## Fun food to draw

Food is friendly, colorful, and easy to make goofy with a quick face.

### A stack of pancakes
Three stacked ovals with a wavy syrup drip down the sides. Add a square of butter on top.

### A melting ice cream
A cone with a scoop and a few drips running down. The messier it looks, the more fun it is.

### A pizza slice
A triangle with a wavy crust edge and round dots for toppings. Add little pepperoni circles.

### A bunch of grapes
A cluster of overlapping circles narrowing to a point, with a small leaf and stem on top.

### A cupcake
A trapezoid wrapper with a swirly frosting dome and a cherry dot on top.

### A boba drink
A tall rounded cup with a straw and a few round dots at the bottom for the boba pearls.

### A watermelon slice
A half-circle with a green rind, a pink inside, and a scatter of little seed dots.

## Fun fantasy ideas

When you want to relax, made-up creatures are the most freeing thing to draw. No reference needed.

### A friendly dragon
A bean-shaped body, a long neck, tiny wings, and a few back spikes. Keep the face round and kind.

### A unicorn
A simple horse head with a spiral horn and a wavy mane. Color the mane in rainbow stripes.

### A cute ghost
A wavy bell shape with two dots for eyes and a small "o" mouth. Let the bottom edge ripple.

### A wizard hat
A tall triangle with a curled tip, a wide brim, and a star or two for sparkle.

### A magic potion
A round flask with a thin neck, a cork, and a few bubbles rising inside. Add a glow line.

### A star creature
A five-point star with two eyes, stubby arms, and a tiny smile. Easy and instantly charming.

### A floating island
A chunk of land with a tree on top and roots dangling below. Add a tiny waterfall off the edge.

## Fun random things to draw

When you want a surprise, mix it up. These **random fun things to draw** keep the page lively.

### A hot air balloon
A big round balloon with a small basket hanging below on two lines. Add stripes for color.

### A paper airplane
A few straight lines folded into a dart shape, with a curvy dotted trail behind it.

### A treasure chest
A rectangle with a curved lid, a round lock, and a few coins or gems peeking out the top.

### A cozy mug
A "U" shape with a handle, a marshmallow or two on top, and wavy steam rising up.

### A vinyl record
A circle with a smaller circle label in the middle and a few thin ring lines around it.

### A retro camera
A rounded rectangle with a big lens circle, a small flash square, and a shutter button on top.

### A roller skate
A boot shape with four round wheels under it and a star on the side. Add speed lines for motion.

## Tips for fun drawing

- Let it be silly. A goofy face turns any plain shape into something that makes you smile.
- Don't erase too much. Wobbly lines have charm, and chasing perfect kills the fun.
- Draw with music on. A good playlist keeps your hand loose and your mood light.
- Try a doodle page. Fill one sheet with tiny drawings instead of one big one.
- Color outside the lines on purpose. Relaxing means breaking your own rules sometimes.

## FAQ

### What are some fun things to draw when bored?
Goofy doodles are the most fun, like a potato with legs, a cat loaf, or a blob monster. They start from one simple shape, take a minute, and almost always end up making you laugh.

### What should I draw to relax?
Draw soft, repetitive shapes like clouds, swirls, or a page of tiny stars. Easy patterns calm your hand and your mind, so there's no pressure to make anything look real.

### How do I make my drawings more fun?
Add a face. A pair of dot eyes and a little smile turns a plain donut, cloud, or rock into a happy character, and that small touch makes the whole drawing more playful.
