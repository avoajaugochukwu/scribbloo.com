Looking for Halloween drawing ideas that are cute and a little spooky? You are in the right spot. This list is loaded with things to draw for Halloween using simple shapes like circles, triangles, and wavy lines. No skill needed. If you can draw a round pumpkin and a wobbly ghost, you can make almost everything here. We cover friendly monsters, jack o lanterns, spooky scenes, sweet candy, and extra cute Halloween doodles. The trick is simple: round shapes feel cute, pointy shapes feel spooky. Grab a pencil and let's make something fun and frightful.

## How to use this list

Pick any idea that grabs you. You do not need to go in order. Each idea names the easy starting shape so you know exactly where to begin. Draw light at first, then darken the lines you love. When you are done, color it in with oranges, purples, and a little black for that classic Halloween glow.

## Friendly monsters

### Wobbly ghost
Start with a bell shape and give the bottom a wavy edge. Add two dot eyes and an open smile.

### One eyed monster
Draw a round blob body. Add one big circle eye, a wide grin, and two stubby arms.

### Furry monster
A tall oval covered in fur lines. Add two horns, big eyes, and silly teeth.

### Little vampire
A round head with slicked hair and a high collar cape. Add two tiny fangs and a smile.

### Friendly witch
A triangle hat on a round head. Add a small broom and a happy face under the brim.

### Mummy buddy
An oval body wrapped in crisscross bandage lines. Leave a gap for two peeking eyes.

### Frankenstein friend
A square head with flat hair on top. Add two neck bolts and a calm smile.

### Goofy zombie
A simple figure with droopy arms out front. Add a stitched smile and one raised eyebrow.

## Pumpkins and jack o lanterns

### Classic jack o lantern
Draw a fat circle and add ridge lines. Cut triangle eyes, a triangle nose, and a toothy grin.

### Round pumpkin
A wide oval with curved lines down the front. Add a small green stem and a leaf.

### Cute smiling pumpkin
A circle pumpkin with dot eyes and a tiny mouth instead of carved holes. Rosy cheeks help.

### Pumpkin stack
Three pumpkins piled up, smallest on top. Add stems and a few fall leaves.

### Spooky pumpkin
Carve sharp zigzag eyes and a jagged mouth. Add glow lines around it.

### Pumpkin patch
A row of pumpkins on a wavy ground line. Add curly vines connecting them.

### Tiny gourd
A bumpy oval with a curved neck. Color it in warm orange and yellow stripes.

### Candle pumpkin
A pumpkin with a soft glow inside. Add little light lines coming from the carved eyes.

## Spooky scenes

### Haunted house
A tall crooked box with pointed roofs. Add dark windows, a leaning door, and a bat in the sky.

### Bare spooky tree
A trunk with twisty bare branches. Add a few hanging leaves and a perched crow.

### Full moon night
A big circle moon with thin clouds drifting across. Add a flying bat shape below.

### Old gravestone
A rounded rectangle in the ground. Add a crack line and a tiny resting ghost on top.

### Creepy cobweb
Lines fanning from a corner with curved threads between them. Add a small spider.

### Spider on a web
Draw an oval body and eight bent legs. Hang it from a single thread line.

### Black cat
An oval body with a round head, pointy ears, and an arched back. Add a curled tail.

### Bat trio
Three rounded bodies with scalloped wings. Space them across the sky like little Ms.

## Halloween candy

### Wrapped candy
An oval with twisted ends on each side. Add stripes and a tiny smile.

### Candy corn
A simple triangle in three color bands: yellow, orange, and white. Quick and classic.

### Lollipop swirl
A circle with a spiral inside on a thin stick. So fun to color.

### Chocolate bar
A rectangle with a peeled wrapper and small square grid lines.

### Candy bucket
A pumpkin shaped pail with a handle. Fill it with little candy shapes peeking out.

### Gummy worm
A wavy tube shape with ridge lines. Color it in two bright stripes.

### Caramel apple
A round apple on a stick with drippy coating. Add a few sprinkle dots.

### Bag of treats
A small pouch with a folded top. Draw candy shapes spilling out the front.

## Cute Halloween doodles

### Smiling ghost
A small rounded ghost with closed happy eyes and rosy cheeks. The cutest of the bunch.

### Tiny bat
A round face with two little wings and pointy ears. Add a sweet smile.

### Happy spider
A round body with short curly legs and big shiny eyes. Hang it from a heart web.

### Candy hearts
A heart shape with a tiny face. Color it in soft Halloween pastels.

### Boo banner
The word boo in bubble letters with little ghost faces inside each O.

### Cute cauldron
A round pot with a smiling face and bubbles floating up from the top.

### Pumpkin cat
A pumpkin body with cat ears and a tail. A fun mashup doodle.

### Witch hat with stars
A triangle hat with a buckle band and tiny stars sprinkled around it.

### Mini broom
A handle with a fan of bristle lines at the bottom. Tie a ribbon at the join.

### Sleepy moon
A crescent moon with closed eyes and a soft smile. Tuck a bat beside it.

These things to draw for Halloween show how shapes set the mood: keep them round for cute, make them jagged for scary.

## Tips

- Round shapes read as cute. Pointy, jagged shapes read as spooky.
- Use orange, purple, and black for that instant Halloween feel.
- Add glow lines around carved pumpkins and the moon to light the scene.
- Give monsters rosy cheeks if you want them friendly instead of frightful.
- Draw light first, then darken the lines you like to keep it tidy.

## FAQ

### What are easy Halloween drawing ideas for beginners?
Round pumpkins and wobbly ghosts are the easiest places to start. Both begin with a basic shape and need just a face. Add candy doodles and bats and your page fills up fast.

### What are some scary Halloween drawing ideas?
For a spooky look, try a haunted house, a bare twisty tree, or a jack o lantern with jagged zigzag eyes. Sharp, pointy lines and a dark night sky make any scene feel creepy.
